About

A bit about me

I'm Nate Alvarez, an MBA candidate at Harvard Business School (Class of 2026) with a passion for building products that solve real problems. I operate at the intersection of technology strategy and hands-on product development, pairing strong product instincts with a technical background.

Before HBS, I was a Mechanical Engineer at Rivian, where I owned exterior trim systems across the R1, R1S, and R2 platforms, engaging cross-functional teams of engineers through design, validation, and mass production. I learned how to ship physical products at scale with zero tolerance for error.

Before Rivian, I was a Mechanical Engineer at Virgin Hyperloop, leading testing and manufacturing of high-voltage electronics systems for next-generation transportation. I also built medical devices at PercuSense, developing multi-analyte body-worn sensor devices.

These portfolio projects represent my approach to product management: identify a genuine user need, prototype rapidly, and iterate with real feedback. Whether it's making intelligence briefings actionable, helping athletes build better workouts, or removing bias from social tasting events. I care about building things that people reach for every day.
